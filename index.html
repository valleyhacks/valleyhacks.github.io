About us

SJHacks is a student-run hackathon targeted and created for high school students in the Bay Area. We plan to create an all inclusive event for students and mentors alike to come together for a weekend and share knowledge about the computer science field.

With our hackathon, we hope to convince students that anyone can learn to code and take one step towards eliminating the “computer nerd” stereotype. We hope to create a sense of community amongst these students in order to break barriers and normalize hacking for all.

The hackathon will offer an environment focused on increasing diversity in the field of computer science. Furthermore, sponsors will have the opportunity to send mentors, present APIs, and evangelize their brands.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is "SJHacks"?

SJHacks aims to be the most social high-school hackathon in the Bay Area. This means 24 straight hours full of workshops, activities, mentors, icebreakers, and most importantly: free swag.

As a team, we want students from all backgrounds to spend 24 hours learning and sharing their knowledge about the computer-science field about everything from blockchain to AI to an app that cleans water bottles; in short, we want everyone to innovate and pioneer while networking to meet new people from all creeds.

tl;dr: The best 24 hours you'll have all summer.
